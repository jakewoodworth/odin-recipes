Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a traditional Italian pasta dish known for its rich, creamy texture made without cream. The sauce is created by combining eggs, cheese, and hot pasta, which come together to form a smooth coating on the noodles. Crispy pork and freshly cracked black pepper add depth and balance to the dish.

This recipe focuses on simple ingredients and quick preparation, making it an ideal meal when you want something comforting yet elegant. When made correctly, carbonara is silky, savory, and deeply satisfying without being heavy.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Bacon or pancetta
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Instructions

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until tender.
  2. While the pasta cooks, cook the bacon until crispy and set it aside.
  3. Lightly cook the garlic in the rendered bacon fat, then remove from heat.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated cheese, and black pepper.
  5. Drain the pasta and immediately combine it with the bacon and garlic.
  6. Remove from heat and stir in the egg and cheese mixture.
  7. Toss until the sauce becomes smooth and coats the pasta evenly.
  8. Serve right away with additional cheese and black pepper if desired.
