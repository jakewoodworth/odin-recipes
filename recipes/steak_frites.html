Steak Frites

Description

Steak frites is a classic dish that pairs a well-seared steak with crispy fries for a simple but satisfying meal. The focus is on high-quality ingredients and proper cooking technique rather than complex preparation. A rich sauce or herb butter is often served alongside the steak to enhance its flavor.

This dish balances crispy, golden fries with tender, juicy steak, making it both hearty and elegant. Steak frites is commonly enjoyed as a restaurant-style meal but can be made at home with minimal ingredients and careful timing.

Ingredients

  • Steak (such as ribeye, strip steak, or flank steak)
  • Potatoes
  • Olive oil or cooking oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic
  • Fresh herbs (such as parsley or thyme)
  • Butter

Instructions

  1. Prepare the potatoes by cutting them into thin strips and soaking or drying them as needed.
  2. Cook the potatoes until golden and crispy, then season with salt.
  3. Season the steak generously with salt and pepper.
  4. Heat a pan until very hot and sear the steak on both sides.
  5. Add butter, garlic, and herbs to the pan and baste the steak.
  6. Cook the steak to the desired doneness, then let it rest.
  7. Serve the steak alongside the fries, finishing with extra herbs or butter if desired.
